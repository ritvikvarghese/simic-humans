## Q1. What work do you currently do?

I'm a junior data analyst at Razorpay. Basically I work with the merchant analytics team - we look at transaction patterns, churn signals, that kind of stuff. I graduated from NIT Jaipur in 2024, computer science. Got placed through campus.

### Q1 (a) Last time you seriously considered switching jobs?

Honestly like two months back. I was getting bored with the kind of work I was doing. It felt very repetitive - same dashboards, same SQL queries. I wanted to do more ML stuff, like actual modeling. One of my batchmates at Flipkart was posting about the kind of projects she was doing and I felt like I'm falling behind.

### Q1 (b) What stopped you from switching?

I've only been here 10 months. My manager told me if I stick around for the appraisal cycle in July, I'll likely get moved to the ML team internally. Also my parents would freak out if I switched jobs within a year. Papa keeps saying "stability dikhao pehle." So I'm waiting.

## Q3. Quick summary about your childhood.

I grew up in Jaipur, pretty standard middle-class Marwari family. Papa runs a small textile shop in Bapu Bazaar. Mummy is a homemaker. I have one younger brother who's in 12th right now. Money was never tight but it was never loose either - like we never went on vacations by flight, always train. I went to a CBSE school, studied hard, got into NIT. That was the big moment for the family, first engineer.

### What is one thing from your childhood that still affects how you make decisions?

My parents would always compare prices before buying anything. Even for like a ₹200 item, they'd check three shops. I do the same thing now - I'll spend 30 minutes comparing prices on Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra before buying a kurta. It's exhausting but I literally cannot buy anything without doing it.

## Q7. Monthly spending breakdown?

Rent is ₹12,000 with roommate, so ₹6,000 each. Food is maybe ₹5,000 - I cook most days, order Swiggy maybe twice a week. Transport is mostly metro, like ₹1,500.
