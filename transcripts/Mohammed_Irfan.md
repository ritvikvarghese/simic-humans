## Q1. What work do you currently do?

I'm an operations manager at Lenskart. I handle the supply chain for their South India warehouses - Hyderabad and Chennai. Before this I was at Delhivery for three years doing similar work. Operations is what I know. I did my MBA from Osmania University, specialized in operations. It's not glamorous work but everything runs because of people like us, that's what I believe.

### Q1 (a) Last time you seriously considered switching?

About four months ago. A friend who works at Meesho offered to refer me for a senior ops role. The pay was almost 30% more. I went through two rounds of interviews.

### Q1 (b) What stopped you?

Lenskart matched most of the hike when I told my manager. Not the full 30% but close enough. Also Meesho's office was in Bangalore and I didn't want to relocate. My parents are here, my daughter is settled in her playschool, my wife's family is here. Hyderabad is home. Moving for 3-4 lakhs more didn't make sense when you factor in Bangalore rent and the cost of being away from family.

## Q3. Quick summary about your childhood.

Born and raised in the Old City area of Hyderabad. Abbu had a small printing press in Charminar. Ammi was a teacher at an Urdu-medium school. We were lower-middle class - comfortable but always careful. I went to a government school till 7th, then got a scholarship to a private school. That was a big shift. Suddenly I was around kids who had Nikes and went to Goa on vacation. I had Bata shoes and went to my nani's house in Warangal.

### One thing from childhood that affects decisions today?

Abbu always said "qarz mat lo" - don't take loans. He ran the printing press for 25 years without a single loan. Even when the machine broke down, he borrowed from family, never a bank. That's stuck with me. I have one credit card - HDFC - I use it only for the cashback and pay the full amount every month. I've never paid interest on a credit card. The idea of EMIs on consumer goods makes me uncomfortable.

## Q5. Marriage and financial decisions.

I got married in 2021. Arranged marriage - our families knew each other through the mosque community. My wife Fatima is a school teacher, makes about ₹25,000 a month. Marriage changed everything. Before marriage, my money was my money. Now it's our money. We have a shared account for household expenses on SBI, and we each keep separate accounts for personal stuff.

### One decision that would've gone differently before marriage?

Last year I wanted to buy a Royal Enfield Classic 350. I'd been wanting one for years. Fatima said it's not practical - we already have a Honda Activa, and with a 3-year-old, a bike is not useful for the family. She said save that ₹2 lakh for Aisha's school admission. Before marriage, I would've just bought it. Now I see her point. We put that money in a recurring deposit instead.

## Q7. Monthly spending breakdown?

We live in my parents' house so no rent, which is a huge advantage. Groceries and household is about ₹12,000-15,000 - joint family so it's higher. Aisha's playschool is ₹4,000. Petrol and transport maybe ₹3,000. I give Ammi ₹10,000 for the house. SIPs are ₹8,000 - ₹5,000 in a flexi-cap on Zerodha and ₹3,000 in a gold fund on Groww. Insurance premiums eat about ₹3,000 a month when you average it out. EMI on home loan - we bought a 2BHK in Kompally last year - that's ₹18,000 a month. That's the only loan I have and even that made me anxious. But Abbu said property is different from consumer debt.

### One thing you'd never cut?

Aisha's expenses. Whatever she needs - school, clothes, doctor visits - that's first priority. Even before SIPs.

## Q12. Religion and daily decisions?

I'd say 7/10. We pray five times, not always but usually four or five. We eat halal only, so that limits where we eat out - we mostly go to restaurants in Tolichowki or the Old City where we know the food is halal. During Ramzan, our whole spending pattern changes - groceries go up because of iftar prep, but we eat out less. I give zakat every year. It's about 2.5% of savings. Last year it was around ₹15,000. That's a fixed annual expense that I plan for.

### Practices you don't follow?

I don't avoid conventional banking even though some in my community believe interest is haram. I've thought about it but practically, there's no real Islamic banking option in India that works. My home loan is from SBI, standard interest. I made peace with it.

## Q17. Governance - agree or disagree?

2 out of 5. Disagree. Look, infrastructure in Hyderabad has gotten better - the ORR, metro, IT corridor, all great. But I feel like certain communities are being sidelined. Waqf board issues, the way some policies target Muslim neighborhoods, it doesn't feel equal. I voted in the last Telangana state election. I tend to lean towards parties that I feel will protect minority rights but I don't want to name names.

### One thing you'd change?

Equal enforcement of law. If there's a demolition drive, it should apply to everyone equally, not just certain areas. That's all I want - fairness.
