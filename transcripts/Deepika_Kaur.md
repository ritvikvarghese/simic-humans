## Q1. What work do you currently do?

I'm a product marketing manager at Zoho. I handle positioning and go-to-market for Zoho CRM in the Indian SMB segment. Before Zoho I was at FreshWorks for two years. I studied at PEC Chandigarh, did electrical engineering, but I hated it. Got into marketing through a campus club where I ran social media for the college fest. Never looked back.

### Q1 (a) Last time you considered switching?

Constantly, honestly. Zoho pays well but the work culture is very, very Chennai. Like everything moves slower than I'd like. I had a conversation with a recruiter from Notion about three weeks ago. They're building out their India marketing team. The role sounded exciting but it was Bangalore-based and I'd just renewed my lease here.

### Q1 (b) What stopped you?

Timing mostly. I have an ESOP vesting in 8 months and I don't want to walk away from that. Also, I'm trying to be less impulsive about career moves. I switched from FreshWorks after just 18 months and I think that hurt me in some interviews. I want to show at least 2.5-3 years at Zoho before I move.

## Q3. Quick summary about your childhood.

I grew up in Sector 22, Chandigarh. Very planned city, very clean, very boring - at least when you're a teenager. Papa is a retired Army Colonel. Mummy ran a boutique for Punjabi suits from home. Money was always comfortable - Army background means housing was sorted, healthcare was sorted. But Papa was strict about spending. No pocket money beyond ₹500 a month even in 12th standard. I learned to be resourceful. I'd resell notes and assignments to juniors for extra cash.

### One thing from childhood that affects decisions?

Papa's discipline. He had a budget for everything - monthly, yearly. I use the same approach. I have a Google Sheet where I track every expense. Not because I'm tight on money but because I like knowing where it's going. Last month I spent ₹2,300 on chai at Third Wave and Blue Tokai and when I saw the number I was like, okay, I need to buy a French press.

## Q5. Relationship and financial decisions.

I've been dating Arjun for about a year and a half. He's a software engineer at Atlassian, lives in Bangalore. Long distance. We don't share finances at all. We split bills when we visit each other. He earns more than me and sometimes tries to pay for everything but I hate that. I insist on splitting. It's a principle thing.

### How would marriage change things?

I think about this. Honestly the part that scares me is the joint family expectation. His parents are in Lucknow and very traditional. They'll expect us to contribute to household expenses, visit frequently, maybe eventually move closer. I don't want to lose my independence. I've been living alone since I was 22 and I love it. But also - a Sikh girl and a Hindu boy, inter-caste, our families haven't fully come around yet. So marriage is not immediate. Maybe in 2-3 years.

## Q7. Monthly spending breakdown?

Rent is ₹18,000 for a 1BHK in Adyar. Groceries and cooking - I cook a lot, Punjabi food mostly - maybe ₹6,000. Eating out and café hopping probably ₹5,000-6,000. Flights to Chandigarh or Bangalore once a month, average ₹4,000 if I book early. SIPs are ₹12,000 - split across three funds on Kuvera. Gym membership at Cult Fit ₹1,500. Clothes and personal care maybe ₹3,000-4,000. I just bought a Dyson air purifier on Bajaj Finserv no-cost EMI, that's ₹2,500 a month for 6 months.

### One thing you'd never cut?

My travel budget. I take one solo trip every quarter - could be Pondicherry for a weekend or Meghalaya for a week. That's my reset button. I'll cut eating out, I'll cut clothes, but I won't cut travel. Last quarter I went to Hampi and spent ₹8,000 total for three days. You don't need a lot of money to travel in India.

## Q8. Savings and investments?

Three SIPs on Kuvera: one large-cap, one mid-cap, one international fund. I also have about ₹1.5 lakh in a Liquid fund for emergencies. No FDs - I don't see the point when liquid funds give similar returns with more flexibility. I've been wanting to start direct equity but I keep procrastinating on opening a Zerodha account.

### What would you never invest in?

Real estate, at least right now. The idea of locking ₹50 lakh into a flat when I'm 28 and might move cities three more times makes zero sense to me. Also LIC policies - Papa has like four and the returns are tragic. I've tried explaining mutual funds to him but he doesn't trust anything that isn't government-backed.

## Q12. Religion and daily decisions?

3/10 on daily decisions. I'm Sikh - I wear a kara, I go to the Gurdwara maybe once a month, I do Ardas before big life events. But I'm not super religious in a ritualistic sense. I don't follow any dietary restrictions from religion - I eat everything including beef, which my parents don't know about. The community aspect matters more to me. When I'm in Chandigarh for Baisakhi or Gurpurab, that feeling of langar and sangat is irreplaceable. But in Chennai, I live a mostly secular daily life.

## Q17. Governance - agree or disagree?

2 out of 5. I think the digital infrastructure - UPI, DigiLocker, Aadhaar - is genuinely impressive. But that's one layer. Women's safety is still terrible. I don't feel safe walking alone at night even in Chennai which is supposed to be one of the safer cities. The farm laws protest in 2020-21 really politicized me - seeing how Punjab was treated felt personal. I voted in 2024 and I align more with parties that talk about federalism and state autonomy. I don't like too much centralization.
