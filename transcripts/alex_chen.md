# Q1. Walk me through how you bought your current laptop.

Honestly? I spent three months agonizing over a $2,800 MacBook Pro decision, reading every Reddit thread, watching YouTube reviews at 1.5x, then bought it the night before a client presentation because my old one crashed. My fiancée Priya keeps pointing out I do this — research for months, then buy in a panic. For work tools I don't blink on spending $200 on a better mechanical keyboard, but I still use the free Trello plan for personal stuff. It's weird.

# Q2. How do you decide what to eat for dinner?

Priya decides. I cook maybe twice a week — same three dishes, a garlic pasta, a Taiwanese beef noodle soup my mom taught me, and whatever's in the fridge. During the week we DoorDash way too much — probably $600 a month, which I know is insane. I always say we'll meal prep on Sunday and we never do. If I'm alone I just eat cereal or skip it.

# Q3. What did you do with your last bonus?

Half went into my Robinhood account, mostly into QQQ because I don't trust myself to pick stocks but I believe in tech long-term. A quarter went to our wedding fund. The rest disappeared on stuff — bought an overpriced standing desk, upgraded my parents to business class for their flight to visit, treated myself to a Theragun I used twice. The spending part doesn't feel like me but it's always what happens.

# Q4. Tell me about a time you changed your mind about something important.

I used to think I'd move back to Taipei eventually to be near my parents. Then in 2023 my dad had a minor stroke and I flew back for two weeks. Being there, I realized I'd become American in ways I couldn't undo — the pace, the food, the loneliness I'd trained myself to tolerate. The thing I feel guilty about: I was relieved to come back to SF. So now I send money monthly and visit twice a year and try not to think about what that means.

# Q5. Who do you call when something goes wrong?

Priya first, always, even when she's the problem. Then my college roommate Deepak — he's a therapist now, which is ironic because I never actually pay him. For work stuff, my VP Marcus; he's the one who recruited me into product from engineering. I notice I don't call my parents when things are bad. I call them when things are good, to perform the son they expect.

# Q6. What's your relationship with money?

I grew up not-poor but my parents talked about money constantly — "we can't afford that" about things we probably could. So now I overcheck my bank account, feel weird about spending, but also just… spend anyway. I have $240k saved, which people say is "good for 32" but I feel broke every time we look at houses in the Bay. I think I'll feel broke forever. That's probably not about money.
