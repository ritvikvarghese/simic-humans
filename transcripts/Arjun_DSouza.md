## Q1. What work do you currently do?

I'm a freelance video editor. I edit for about four YouTube channels — two tech channels, one travel creator, and one fitness guy. That's my bread and butter, brings in about ₹30,000-40,000 a month depending on how many videos they upload. On top of that I run my own YouTube channel about Mangalorean food and culture — that has about 18,000 subscribers. The channel doesn't make real money yet, maybe ₹2,000-3,000 from AdSense. But that's the dream — to make the channel my main thing eventually.

### Q1 (a) Last time you considered getting a regular job?

Literally every month when rent is due. Like January was terrible — two of my clients didn't upload anything for three weeks because of New Year breaks. I made ₹18,000 the whole month. My roommate who works at Infosys was like, "bro just apply, they're hiring editors for their internal comms team, ₹45,000 fixed." I actually went to the interview.

### Q1 (b) What stopped you?

I got the offer but the work was corporate videos. Like, internal town halls, training modules. I sat with the offer letter for two days and I just couldn't. I kept thinking, I left Mangalore to do creative work, not to make PowerPoint recordings look cinematic. The money would've been nice but I think I'd have been miserable within three months. My mum was upset. She said "at least you'd have EPF and health insurance." She's not wrong.

## Q3. Quick summary about your childhood.

Mangaluru. Catholic family, Konkani-speaking. Daddy works at MRPL — the Mangalore Refinery. Good stable government-adjacent job. Mummy is a teacher at St. Agnes School. I went to St. Aloysius for school. Very typical Mangalorean Catholic upbringing — church every Sunday, catechism classes, Christmas was the biggest event of the year. Daddy is from a farming family originally — they have some land in Bantwal with arecanut and coconut. I spent every summer vacation there as a kid.

### One thing from childhood that affects decisions?

We always had a surplus mentality because of the land. Like even if money was tight, there was always rice from the paddy, coconut from the farm, pickle that grandma made. I think that's why I'm okay with income fluctuation as a freelancer — I have this deep belief that things will work out, there's always something to fall back on. My friends from Delhi or Mumbai who freelance seem way more anxious about irregular income.

## Q5. Single — how do you think marriage will change things?

I don't think about marriage much right now. I'm 25, my parents aren't pressuring yet — Mangalorean families usually start the "boy-seeing" process around 27-28. I know when it happens, it'll probably be within the community — Catholic Mangalorean girl. That's what my parents want and honestly I'm okay with it. The wedding will be expensive though. Mangalorean Catholic weddings are massive — 500+ people minimum. Daddy has been saving for it separately, he told me that once. I'd rather do a small wedding and use that money for something else but I know that's not happening. It's not just a wedding, it's a family event for both sides.

### Would you spend a lot on the wedding?

If I'm paying, no. If Daddy is paying, I don't think I have a say. He'll invite everyone from the parish, all the relatives from Bantwal, his MRPL colleagues. My cousin's wedding last year had 700 people and cost ₹12 lakh. That's the benchmark.

## Q7. Monthly spending breakdown?

Rent is ₹15,000 for a 3BHK in Koramangala split three ways, so ₹5,000 each. Food is ₹6,000-7,000 — I cook Mangalorean food a lot, fish curry, neer dosa. Fish in Bangalore is expensive compared to Mangalore, that annoys me. Internet is ₹1,200 — I need fast internet for uploading and rendering. Chai and cafés, honestly ₹2,000-3,000. I work from cafés a lot. Equipment EMI — I bought a MacBook Air M2 on Bajaj EMI, that's ₹4,000 a month, 6 months left. Savings — when I have a good month, I try to put ₹5,000-10,000 in my savings. When it's bad, zero.

### One thing you'd never cut?

Internet and my Adobe Creative Cloud subscription — ₹1,700 a month. That's literally my livelihood. Without Premiere Pro and After Effects I can't work. Some people say use DaVinci Resolve for free but I've built my entire workflow on Adobe and switching would cost me more time than money.

## Q8. Savings and investments?

Honestly this is embarrassing. I have about ₹80,000 in my Kotak savings account. That's it. No SIPs, no FDs, no mutual funds. I keep meaning to start a SIP on Groww — I downloaded the app six months ago and still haven't completed KYC. My income is too variable to commit to a fixed monthly SIP, or at least that's what I tell myself. I know it's an excuse. I could do even ₹1,000 a month.

### What would you never invest in?

Stock trading. My roommate Karthik does intraday trading on Zerodha and I've seen him lose ₹8,000 in a single afternoon. He acts like it's fine but that's like a week of my income. I can't handle that kind of volatility. If I ever invest, it'll be something boring and safe. Maybe a recurring deposit to start.

## Q12. Religion and daily decisions?

4/10. I go to church maybe twice a month — there's a nice one in Koramangala. I don't eat meat on Fridays during Lent, that's a habit from childhood. Christmas and Easter I go home to Mangalore without fail. I wear a cross pendant that my grandmother gave me — haven't taken it off in years. But I'm not very devout day-to-day. I don't pray daily or read the Bible. Religion for me is more about community and family than personal practice. When I'm home, I go to church with the family. In Bangalore, it's more flexible.

### Practice you don't follow?

The Church says no living together before marriage. Most of my friends, Catholic or not, their parents would flip if they knew they're living with a partner. I don't have a girlfriend right now but when I did, she stayed over regularly. I don't think the Church should have a say in that.

## Q17. Governance — agree or disagree?

2 out of 5. I think the basics are still broken. I go to a government hospital, the experience is terrible. I try to file taxes as a freelancer and the process is confusing. GST for freelancers makes no sense when I'm making ₹4 lakh a year. I didn't vote in the last national election — I was too lazy to transfer my voter ID from Mangalore to Bangalore. I regret it. I'll do it before the next one. Politically I don't have strong leanings. I just want basic governance — roads, hospitals, less corruption.

### One thing you'd change?

Tax freelancers fairly. I'm a 25-year-old making ₹4 lakh and the tax system treats me like I'm hiding income because I don't have a Form 16. The whole system is designed for salaried people. Self-employed people are an afterthought.
